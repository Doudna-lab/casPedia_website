WHAT IS CasPEDIA?

CasPEDIA is an encyclopedia of Class II CRISPR systems, presented in wiki format. Wiki entries provide comprehensive descriptions of enzyme activities, structures and sequences, complete with a literature review covering each nuclease's discovery, experimental considerations and applications. CasPEDIA exists in tandem with CRISPRpedia, a free resource created by The Innovative Genomics Institute that describes the principles of CRISPR technology.

CasPEDIA introduces CasIDs, which organize Cas enzymes by their functional properties. Inspired by the ENZYME Classification (E.C.) system

, CasID nomenclature is comprised of three digits, described below, that concisely describe effector properties and enable rapid identification of appropriate enzyme for a researcher's use case. CasIDs can be used to navigate the website through direct search in the search bar and using our Tool Finder

Please visit the FAQ section for directions on navigating CasPEDIA and information about becoming a curator.

DEFINING Cas IDs

CasIDs are comprised of three digits, which represent nuclease activity, target requirement, and guide RNA (gRNA) design, respectively. Definitions for all CasID digits are provided below.

To demonstrate CasIDs, consider SpyCas9a, with a CasID of 1.1.1 . The Nuclease Activity for this enzyme falls under category 1, implying it has blunt double-stranded cis nuclease-activity, and no trans nuclease-activity (1. The Targeting Requirements for ShomepyCas9a fit category 1, meaning it requires a 3' protospacer-adjacent motif (PAM). Finally, the Guide RNA (gRNA) Design and Multiplexing properties for this enzyme fall under category 1, such that the Native CRISPR Array for SpyCas9a requires a CRISPR RNA (crRNA) for targeting + Trans-acting crRNA (tracrRNA) for multiplexing to the protein backbone of the effector + additional factors for processing the array into mature guides. However, as described by the CasID nomenclature, SpyCas9a can also be engineered to utilize a minimal array containing different single-guide RNAs (sgRNAs), represented as a contiguous, all-in-one crRNA+tracrRNA sequence2,3.

Nuclease Activity

Nuclease activity is reported as the first digit in the Cas ID. Enzymes from the Class II Cas superfamily have diverse nuclease activities, both in an RNA-guided manner on a targeted sequence (in cis-) and sometimes on non-targeted nucleic acids upon binding (in trans-). The numerical annotation for nuclease activity relates to the known nucleic acid substrates for both the cis- and trans- activities of these enzymes.

Target Requirement

Targeting requirement is reported as the second digit in the Cas ID. Target requirement refers to the orientation of the Cas enzyme relative to its RNA-guided target substrate, as dictated by its protospacer adjacent motif (PAM), for DNA substrates, and protospacer flanking site (PFS), for RNA. The numerical annotation shows the orientation of the PAM, or PFS, relative to the protospacer and dictates the orientation of the ribonucleoprotein (RNP) relative to targeted substrate.

Guide RNA (gRNA) design + Multiplexing

Multiplexability is reported as the third digit in the Cas ID. Guide RNA (gRNA) design illustrates the process bridging CRISPR native context through synthetic gRNA scaffold design. The numerical annotation describes the relevant guide requirements in relevant several contexts: within the native CRISPR, the minimal synthetic guide design for multiplexing, and the sgRNA design for multiplexing.
