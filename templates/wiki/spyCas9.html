DESCRIPTION

SUMMARY

The discovery of SpyCas9 ((10.1126/science.1225829)) from Streptococcus pyogenes, and consequent demonstration of its mammalian genome editing ((10.1126/science.1232033)), initiated a revolution in fundamental biology and therapeutic development. SpyCas9 is the most broadly utilized and validated CRISPR editor and has been shown to be highly adept at modifying the genome of many organisms, ranging from phage to humans.

APPLICATIONS

Administrating Cas9 with a guide, either as a sgRNA or crRNA bound to a tracrRNA, has a number of biotechnological applications. CRISPR-based treatments are currently under investigation in human clinical trials, including ex vivo edited cell therapies and systemic in vivo editing in patients. Additionally, by leveraging CRISPR-Cas9â€™s ability to introduce targeted genetic disruptions, scientists have built successful platforms for the rapid creation of knockout animal models, genetic screening, multiplexed editing and diagnostic testing. CRISPR-Cas9 has also been harnessed for agricultural purposes, improving the plant immunity and nutrition content in for staple crops like wheat and rice.

Engineers have also developed new variants for Cas9 with expanded properties, including novel PAMs, higher efficacy and improved thermostability. A number of fusion proteins have also been generated with CRISPR-Cas9, including base-editors and prime editors, which are capabale of generating site-specific mutations without the introduction of double-stranded breaks.
